Activity 2

The prompting pattern

Strong prompts give Copilot a clear goal, relevant context, the sources it should use, and clear expectations for the response.

GoalContextSourceExpectations

Worked example

Summarize the three most important performance risks for my upcoming regional business review. Focus on issues that require a leadership decision this week. Use the attached store performance workbook as the source. Format the response as a concise executive briefing with evidence and recommended actions.
  • State the goal clearly: what do you want Copilot to accomplish?
  • Add context about the audience, situation, people, or time period.
  • Identify the email, meeting, file, or other source Copilot should use.
  • Set expectations for format, tone, length, and what to prioritize.
  • Verify facts, numbers, and names before acting or sharing.
Activity 3 · 7 minutes

Catch up: find the signal.

Use Microsoft 365 Copilot Chat to synthesize recent work context, then narrow the response to decisions and risks.

  1. Set your scope. Replace the bracketed areas with the functions or stakeholders you own.
  2. Run the prompt. Review the sources Copilot provides and open at least one supporting item.
  3. Challenge the result. Ask what is uncertain, contradictory, or missing.

Leadership signal scan

Review my work from the past five business days related to [operations, customers, products, people, finance, or another area]. Identify the five developments most likely to require executive attention. For each, explain what changed, why it matters now, the decision or intervention that may be needed, and the source supporting the finding. Separate confirmed facts from possible implications.

Pressure-test follow-up

Which of these findings has the weakest evidence or conflicting signals? Show me what I should verify before acting.

Reflection: Did Copilot surface a decision, or merely organize information?

Activity 4 · 7 minutes

Prepare: enter informed.

Use Copilot with an upcoming meeting and the content available to you in Teams or Outlook. The goal is not a generic agenda—it is a sharper leadership posture.

Point Copilot to the meeting: In Copilot Chat, make sure Work IQ is on. Select Add work content in the prompt box, or type /. Choose Meetings, search by meeting title or organizer, and select the meeting. It will appear as a reference in the prompt.
  1. Choose a real upcoming meeting and add it to the prompt using the Work IQ steps above.
  2. Run the preparation prompt with the meeting reference attached.
  3. Verify participant positions before treating them as fact.

Leadership meeting pre-read

Using the meeting I added to this prompt, prepare me for the discussion. Summarize the purpose, recent developments connected to the agenda, and any decisions that appear to be needed. Identify the participants whose input will be important, but do not infer a person's position unless it is supported by available content. End with three questions I should ask and two assumptions the group should test.

Engagement moment: Pair up and compare the questions Copilot proposed. Which one would materially improve the meeting?

Activity 5 · 12 minutes

Analyze: test the story.

Move from communication signals to business evidence. Choose one route; each uses a different dataset and a prompt designed for that tool and business question.

Copilot in ExcelOpen the performance workbook in Excel to compare regions, store groups, and products.

Copilot in Excel prompt

Analyze this weekly store and product performance data to identify where leadership attention would have the greatest impact this week. Compare regions, store groups, and products; identify meaningful outliers and trends; and show the evidence behind each finding. Recommend three actions and the additional information needed before making a final decision. Create a visualization that makes the most important comparison easy to understand.

AnalystOpen Analyst in Microsoft 365 Copilot and attach the customer feedback data to identify recurring themes and intervention priorities.

Analyst prompt

Analyze this customer feedback data to identify the issues most likely to require executive intervention. Group comments into recurring themes, quantify how often each theme appears, identify the stores, regions, products, or service moments most affected, and include representative evidence. Recommend the three intervention priorities with the expected customer or operational benefit of each. Clearly identify limitations or missing context in the data.

Optional · Bring your own data

Analyze the attached [workbook or CSV] to help me decide [the business decision]. The data covers [time period, population, and business process]. First describe the fields and any data-quality limitations you observe. Then identify the most important trends, exceptions, risks, and opportunities; support each finding with evidence; and recommend three actions. Clearly distinguish what the data proves from what would require additional context.

Pressure-test either route

Argue against your leading recommendation. What alternative explanation, missing data, or risk could change the decision?
Required final step: After reviewing and correcting the analysis, run the handoff prompt below. This creates the common input used in Activity 6, regardless of which route or dataset you chose.

Create the Activity 6 handoff

Create a Decision Brief Handoff from this analysis. Include: the decision question; three findings supported by the source data; the evidence for each finding; the recommended action; two risks or counterarguments; and unresolved questions. Keep it under 350 words. Mark any unsupported or uncertain item as Needs validation.

Activity 7 · 6 minutes

Follow through: make it accountable.

Point Copilot Chat to a specific past meeting, convert the discussion into explicit commitments, and open a ready-to-review follow-up draft in Outlook.

Add the specific meeting: In Copilot Chat, make sure Work IQ is on. Select Add work content, or type /. Choose Meetings, search by meeting title or organizer, and select the meeting before pasting the prompt.

Decision record and Outlook follow-up

Using the meeting I added to this prompt, create a decision and owner record from the available recap or transcript. Include decisions made, action items with the named owner and stated due date, unresolved questions, and items discussed but not decided. Do not invent an owner or deadline; mark it as unassigned or not specified. Then draft an Outlook email to the attendees with the decisions first, owners and dates second, and open questions last. End by asking recipients to correct anything that does not match their understanding.

Open the draft in Outlook

Make the email concise and executive-ready. Use a clear subject line and keep the body under 250 words.
  1. Review the generated email card in Copilot Chat and confirm the recipients, subject, decisions, owners, and dates.
  2. Select Open to continue editing the generated draft in Outlook.
  3. Edit and send only after confirming the draft against the meeting record.
Availability note: Use a meeting that was transcribed or recorded so Copilot can ground the recap and follow-up in the meeting content.
Activity 8 · 5 minutes

Lead responsibly.

Copilot accelerates preparation and production. Executive accountability remains with the person who acts on the output.

VerifyOpen sources and confirm names, dates, numbers, and quotations.
SeparateDistinguish facts, interpretations, assumptions, and recommendations.
ProtectUse content only within its intended permissions and business context.
ChallengeAsk for counterarguments, missing evidence, and alternate explanations.
EditApply your judgment to tone, audience, and organizational implications.
OwnThe decision and communication remain yours—not Copilot's.
