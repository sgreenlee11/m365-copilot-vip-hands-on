Welcome and objectives

Activity 2

The prompting pattern

Strong prompts give Copilot a clear goal, relevant context, the sources it should use, and clear expectations for the response.

GoalContextSourceExpectations

Worked example

Summarize the three most important performance risks for my upcoming regional business review. Focus on issues that require a leadership decision this week. Use the attached store performance workbook as the source. Format the response as a concise executive briefing with evidence and recommended actions.
  • State the goal clearly: what do you want Copilot to accomplish?
  • Add context about the audience, situation, people, or time period.
  • Identify the email, meeting, file, or other source Copilot should use.
  • Set expectations for format, tone, length, and what to prioritize.
  • Verify facts, numbers, and names before acting or sharing.
Required exercise

Weekly Digest

Start every week with a clear, organized picture of what happened and what needs your attention.

Best for: Monday morning or after time away Grounding: email, Teams, meetings, files

Prompt to copy

Summarize my past week across email, Teams chats, meetings, and shared files. Group the summary by stakeholder theme: [list the stakeholder groups or focus areas you own -- for example customers, field and operations, product, supply chain, people, finance, and urgent decisions]. For each theme, include what changed, why it matters, any decision needed from me, and suggested next step.

Follow-up prompt

Turn this into a 5-bullet executive briefing I could review before my next leadership meeting.

Reflection: Which theme surfaced something you would have otherwise missed this week?

Required exercise

Meeting Prep

Walk into your next meeting prepared, with the context and the right questions in hand.

Best for: 15-30 minutes before a meeting Grounding: calendar, email, Teams, documents

Add the specific meeting first: In Copilot Chat, make sure Work IQ is on. Select Add work content in the prompt box, or type / to open the Context IQ menu. Choose Meetings, search by meeting title or organizer, and select the meeting you want Copilot to use. The meeting is added to your prompt as a reference; then paste the prompt below.

Prompt to copy

Using the meeting I added to this prompt, prepare me for the discussion. Summarize the agenda, list the key participants and their relevant perspectives, and surface recent emails, Teams messages, or documents connected to the topics we will discuss. Clearly distinguish confirmed information from inferred context. End with the top three questions I should ask.

Follow-up prompt

Create a concise pre-read with sections: Context, Likely Tensions, Decisions Needed, Questions to Ask, and Risks.

Reflection: Did the pre-read change how you plan to open or steer the meeting?

Required exercise

Meeting Decisions and Owners

Turn a meeting into a crisp, shareable record of decisions and commitments before people leave the room.

Best for: end of meeting or immediately after Grounding: Teams recap, notes, transcript

Prompt to copy

From [name the meeting, or paste its notes or transcript], generate the decisions-and-owners block I can post to the chat right now. Include decisions made as specific commitments, action items with named owner and due date, open questions we are carrying forward, and the one decision we explicitly chose not to make today and when we will revisit it.

Follow-up prompt

Rewrite this as a concise Teams post with a professional executive tone.

Reflection: Was any decision less clear than you assumed once you saw it written as a commitment?

Option 4B

Role-Based Catch-Up

Scope the catch-up tightly to your own function and sort by what needs action.

Prompt

Summarize communications from the past week related to [my function -- your area of ownership]. Prioritize anything requiring my decision, anything with customer/store/associate impact, anything with financial or operational risk, and anything that has a deadline. Group the results into: decide today, delegate, monitor, and ignore.

Follow-up

Draft three follow-up messages: one decision response, one delegation note, and one acknowledgement.

Wrap

Responsible use reminder

Copilot accelerates your work, but you remain accountable for the outcome.

  • Review outputs before you act on or share them.
  • Check sources and citations behind key facts and figures.
  • Do not paste sensitive content where it does not belong.
  • Use only approved systems and approved data.
  • Copilot respects permissions, but you own the judgment and accountability.
