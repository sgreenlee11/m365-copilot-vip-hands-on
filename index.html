Prompting pattern

Role + Context + Task + Output format + Constraints

Strong prompts tell Copilot who you are, what situation you are in, what you need done, how the answer should be formatted, and what to prioritize. 

I am a regional operations leader preparing for a business review. Summarize the attached store performance data, identify the three most important risks, and format the answer as an executive briefing with recommended actions.

Hands-on sequence

Complete three required exercises, then choose one track.

Required

Exercise 1: Weekly Digest

When to use it: Monday morning, after time away, or whenever you need to get back on top of what moved.

Summarize my past week across email, Teams chats, meetings, and shared files. Group the summary by stakeholder theme: [list the stakeholder groups or focus areas you own -- for example customers, field and operations, product, supply chain, people, finance, and urgent decisions]. For each theme, include what changed, why it matters, any decision needed from me, and suggested next step.

Follow-up:

Turn this into a 5-bullet executive briefing I could review before my next leadership meeting.

Reflection: Which theme surfaced something you would have otherwise missed this week?

Required

Exercise 2: Meeting Prep

When to use it: 15 to 30 minutes before a meeting when you need the agenda, participants, and relevant history.

Prepare me for [my next meeting -- or name a specific upcoming meeting]. Summarize the agenda, list the key participants and their likely positions, and surface any recent emails, Teams messages, or documents relevant to the topics we will discuss. End with the top three questions I should ask.

Follow-up:

Create a concise pre-read with sections: Context, Likely Tensions, Decisions Needed, Questions to Ask, and Risks.

Reflection: Did the pre-read change how you plan to open or steer the meeting?

Required

Exercise 3: Meeting Decisions and Owners

When to use it: At the end of a meeting, or right after, when you need a clean action record.

From [name the meeting, or paste its notes or transcript], generate the decisions-and-owners block I can post to the chat right now. Include decisions made as specific commitments, action items with named owner and due date, open questions we are carrying forward, and the one decision we explicitly chose not to make today and when we will revisit it.

Follow-up:

Rewrite this as a concise Teams post with a professional executive tone.

Reflection: Was any decision less clear than you assumed once you saw it written as a commitment?

Choose 4A

Store and Product Performance

When to use it: When you have performance data and need the story: what is working, what is not, and what leadership should do.

Using the attached weekly store and product performance data, identify the most important trends, opportunities, and risks. Show the top-performing and underperforming store groups, products, and regions. Recommend the three actions leadership should consider this week, and include the evidence behind each recommendation.

Use with: Demo_Weekly_Store_Product_Performance.xlsx and optionally Demo_Customer_Feedback.csv.

Follow-up:

Convert this into a one-page leadership brief with sections: Situation, Insight, Decision Needed, Risks, and Recommended Next Step.

Reflection: Which insight would you bring to leadership first, and why?

Choose 4B

Role-Based Catch-Up

When to use it: When you want a catch-up scoped tightly to your function, sorted by what you must act on.

Summarize communications from the past week related to [my function -- your area of ownership]. Prioritize anything requiring my decision, anything with customer/store/associate impact, anything with financial or operational risk, and anything that has a deadline. Group the results into: decide today, delegate, monitor, and ignore.

Follow-up:

Draft three follow-up messages: one decision response, one delegation note, and one acknowledgement.

Reflection: What landed in "ignore" that normally consumes your attention?

Responsible use

Copilot accelerates the work. You own the judgment.

  • Review outputs before you act on or share them.
  • Check sources and citations behind key facts and figures.
  • Do not paste sensitive content where it does not belong.
  • Use only approved systems and approved data.
  • Copilot respects your permissions, but you remain accountable for the outcome.
